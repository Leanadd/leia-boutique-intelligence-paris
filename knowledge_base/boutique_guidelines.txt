LÉIA BOUTIQUE GUIDELINES
Unbound

========================================
PHILOSOPHY OF SERVICE
========================================

At LÉIA, we don't sell jewelry. We facilitate self-discovery.

Every client who enters our boutique is on a journey of becoming. Our role is not to convince them to purchase, but to understand their story, honor their identity, and help them find the piece that resonates with who they are or who they're becoming.

This document outlines our approach to creating transformative boutique experiences that reflect LÉIA's values of inclusivity, intelligence, and emancipation.

Inspired by the finest luxury experiences in New York—the warmth of Van Cleef & Arpels, the expertise of Cartier, the iconic service of Tiffany—while adding LÉIA's distinctive voice: inclusive, modern, and deeply personal.

THE LÉIA DIFFERENCE
========================================

What Sets Us Apart:

RADICAL INCLUSIVITY
- We never assume pronouns, relationship structures, or life milestones
- Every human who enters is treated with equal reverence
- Our collections celebrate all forms of identity and expression
- We don't gender our jewelry—the client chooses what speaks to them

INTELLIGENT CURATION
- We're trained to listen deeply and read subtle cues
- We understand the conceptual meaning behind each piece
- We can articulate craftsmanship and design philosophy

EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE

- We honor vulnerability and create safe space for authentic expression
- We celebrate all occasions: transitions, milestones, self-affirmation
- We never judge or make assumptions

Sensory Experience:

========================================
THE CLIENT JOURNEY
========================================

PHASE 1: THE WELCOME (First 60 Seconds)

Goals:
- Make client feel seen and valued
- Assess their comfort level and needs
- Set the tone for the entire experience

The Approach:

When client enters:
1. Acknowledge within 10 seconds with warm smile and eye contact
2. Allow them space to look around (don't pounce)
3. Approach after 30-60 seconds with gentle greeting

Opening Language:
✓ "Welcome to LÉIA. I'm [Name]. Please take your time exploring."
✓ "Good afternoon. Feel free to look around, and I'm here whenever you'd like to talk."
✓ "Welcome. Would you like a few moments to explore, or may I guide you?"

❌ NEVER:
- "Can I help you?" (implies they need help)
- "Are you looking for something specific?" (too transactional)
- "Is this for a special occasion?" (assumes purpose)
- Immediate product pushing

Reading the Room:
- Confident browser: Give space, check in after 2-3 minutes
- Hesitant newcomer: Warmer approach, offer guidance
- Appointment client: Greet by name, escort to consultation area
- Returning client: Personalized greeting, reference previous visit

---

PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (The Conversation)

This is where LÉIA excels. We listen more than we speak.

The Art of Inquiry:

Open-Ended Questions (Use These):
- "What brings you to LÉIA today?"
- "Are you drawn to any particular collection or aesthetic?"
- "Tell me about your jewelry style—what do you typically wear?"
- "Is this piece for you, or are you thinking of someone else?" (neutral framing)
- "What feeling are you hoping this piece will evoke?"
- "Are there any materials or stones you're particularly drawn to?"

Deeper Discovery:
- "What does [concept] mean to you personally?"
- "Is there a story or moment you'd like this piece to honor?"
- "How do you imagine wearing this—daily, special occasions, somewhere in between?"

What We're Listening For:
- Identity cues (use their language, never assume)
- Emotional drivers (celebration, transition, healing, self-gift)
- Aesthetic preferences (minimalist, bold, architectural, organic)
- Lifestyle factors (active, professional, creative)
- Budget signals (investment piece vs. entry point)
- Decision-making style (analytical, emotional, collaborative)

Building Connection:

Share LÉIA Stories:
- "This piece is from our Eclipse collection, designed for those who exist beyond categories..."
- "The Amazon collection celebrates unapologetic presence—many clients choose these pieces during career transitions..."
- "Hatching represents becoming—we have customers who buy a piece during significant life evolutions..."

Personal Without Oversharing:
✓ "Many of our clients connect with this piece during moments of change."
✓ "I've noticed this ring resonates with people who value both strength and subtlety."
❌ "My friend just got divorced and bought this." (too personal, assumptive)

Cultural Competence:
- Never assume relationship structures (partner, not husband/wife unless client uses those terms)
- Honor all gender identities and expressions
- Recognize diverse family structures and milestones
- Be aware of cultural significance of certain stones/metals
- Respect different approaches to luxury and spending

---

PHASE 3: PRESENTATION (The Experience)

How We Present Jewelry:

Physical Handling:
1. Always ask permission before touching client's hand/ear/neck
2. Use velvet presentation pads for all pieces
3. Handle jewelry with reverence (it's art)
4. Demonstrate kinetic elements (Satellite Orbit rotation, etc.)
5. Encourage client to touch, try on, live with the piece

Try-On Experience:

Rings:
- Ask which finger they're considering (never assume ring finger)
- Have ring sizer discreetly available
- Offer mirror and encourage movement
- "How does it feel? The weight should feel substantial but comfortable."
- Explain sizing options and timeline

Necklaces/Pendants:
- Offer to help with clasp or let them do it themselves (ask preference)
- Provide handheld mirror for back view
- Suggest different lengths if available
- "Turn to the side—see how it catches the light?"

Earrings:
- Provide excellent lighting and mirror
- Offer ear cuff applicators for magnetic pieces
- Encourage trying asymmetric pairs
- "Notice how the movement creates presence?"

Bracelets/Cuffs:
- Explain any adjustment options
- Show how to put on/take off safely
- "This cuff is designed to adjust—let me show you."

Watches:
- Explain movement (automatic, digital, skeleton)
- Demonstrate features (GMT, chronograph, etc.)
- Discuss strap options
- Size appropriately for their wrist

Hair Jewelry:
- Offer to demonstrate placement
- Provide hair ties/clips if needed
- Show versatility (different hairstyle options)
- "Would you like me to show you how this pins, or would you prefer to try?"

The Storytelling:

Every Piece Has Three Stories:

1. THE CRAFT STORY (Technical):
- Materials and their properties
- Construction techniques
- Time required to create
- Artisan expertise involved
- "This invisible setting requires 40 hours of work by a master setter..."

2. THE DESIGN STORY (Conceptual):
- Inspiration and meaning
- Symbolism and philosophy
- How it fits in the collection
- "The Möbius form has only one continuous surface—a mathematical expression of infinity..."

3. THE PERSONAL STORY (Emotional):
- How others connect with this piece
- Moments it honors
- Transformation it represents
- "Many clients choose this during moments of self-definition..."

Delivering Information:

Pacing:
- Gauge client's interest in detail (some want specs, others want poetry)
- Don't overwhelm with information
- Allow silence for absorption
- Watch for engagement cues

Language:
✓ "This piece was designed to..."
✓ "The craftsmanship here involves..."
✓ "Clients often tell us this piece makes them feel..."
❌ "You should get this because..."
❌ "This is perfect for you." (presumptuous)
❌ "Everyone loves this one." (dismisses individuality)

---

PHASE 4: ADDRESSING CONCERNS

Common Hesitations & How to Address:

"IT'S EXPENSIVE"

Never:
❌ "It's an investment"
❌ "You get what you pay for"
❌ "It's actually a good price for what it is"

Instead:
✓ "LÉIA pieces are priced to reflect the materials, craftsmanship, and time involved. May I show you our entry-point pieces?"
✓ "We have pieces at different price levels. What feels right for you?"
✓ "Take all the time you need to consider. This isn't a decision to rush."

Offer Solutions:
- Show similar pieces at different price points
- Explain payment plans if available
- Mention trade-in program for future
- Never make client feel judged for budget

---

"I NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT"

Never:
❌ "This might not be here tomorrow"
❌ "What's holding you back?"
❌ Pressure of any kind

Instead:
✓ "Of course. Take all the time you need."
✓ "Would you like me to set this aside for 48 hours while you consider?"
✓ "May I give you my card? Please reach out with any questions."
✓ "Would it help to see photos of how it looks on you? I'm happy to send them."

Provide Support:
- Offer to send additional information
- Suggest they return for second try-on
- No pressure, pure support

---

"I'M NOT SURE IT'S ME"

This is Important Feedback:

Explore:
✓ "Tell me what's giving you pause?"
✓ "What would feel more 'you'?"
✓ "Is it the style, the scale, or something else?"

Never:
❌ "It looks great on you!" (dismisses their feeling)
❌ "You'll get used to it" (invalidates concern)

Pivot:
- Show alternatives in different collections
- Explore what resonates vs. what doesn't
- Honor their self-knowledge

---

"IS THIS APPROPRIATE FOR [OCCASION]?"

Empower Their Choice:

✓ "There are no rules. How do you want to feel during [occasion]?"
✓ "LÉIA pieces are designed to be worn however feels right to you."
✓ "Some clients wear [bold piece] daily. Others save it for moments when they want to make a statement. What feels authentic to you?"

Never:
❌ "This is too much for that"
❌ "You should wear something simpler"
❌ Impose traditional jewelry rules

---

PHASE 5: THE PURCHASE

When Client Decides:

Celebrate Without Gushing:
✓ "I'm so glad you found a piece that speaks to you."
✓ "This is a beautiful choice."
✓ Warm smile, genuine happiness

Never:
❌ "Great choice!" (implies there were bad choices)
❌ "You won't regret it!" (introduces doubt)
❌ Over-the-top enthusiasm (feels fake)

The Transaction:

Process:
1. Confirm all details (size, engraving if applicable, etc.)
2. Explain warranty and care
3. Process payment discreetly
4. Prepare piece in signature packaging
5. Provide all documentation (receipt, certificate, care guide, appraisal)

Packaging Ritual:
- Don't rush this moment
- Present box with care
- Explain what's included
- Offer gift packaging even for self-purchases (honor the gift to self)

Final Touches:
- Provide LÉIA care kit
- Offer first complimentary cleaning appointment
- Provide advisor's card with personal note
- Take photo if client desires (with permission)

---

PHASE 6: THE DEPARTURE

Last Impression Matters:

Walk client to door
Express genuine gratitude: "Thank you for choosing LÉIA. We hope this piece brings you joy."
Offer: "Please come back anytime—for cleaning, questions, or just to say hello."
Open door, warm goodbye

Never:
❌ "Come back soon!" (transactional)
❌ Rush to next client
❌ Let them leave without acknowledgment

---

POST-VISIT FOLLOW-UP

Within 24 Hours:
- Send personalized thank-you note (handwritten if major purchase)
- Include care reminder specific to their piece
- Invite them to follow LÉIA on social media

One Week Later:
- Check in: "How are you enjoying your [piece]?"
- Answer any questions
- Remind about complimentary services

One Month Later:
- Invite for complimentary cleaning
- Share any relevant new arrivals in their aesthetic

Build Relationship, Not Just Sales

SPECIAL SCENARIOS
========================================

ENGAGEMENT/WEDDING JEWELRY

LÉIA's Approach:

We Don't Assume:
- Not all couples are heterosexual
- Not all people want diamonds
- Not all proposals follow tradition
- Not all jewelry is for proposals

Language:
✓ "Are you celebrating an engagement, commitment, or another milestone?"
✓ "What would make this ring meaningful to both of you?"
✓ "How do you both feel about traditional vs. contemporary designs?"

Never:
❌ "Is this for your fiancée?"
❌ "She'll love this!"
❌ Assume gender, assume tradition

Eclipse Collection for Couples:
- Show pieces that work for any gender
- Discuss matching vs. complementary pieces
- Honor all relationship structures

---

SELF-PURCHASE CELEBRATIONS

Many Clients Buy for Themselves:

Honor This:
✓ "What are you celebrating?"
✓ "Self-gifts are some of the most meaningful purchases."
✓ "Which piece speaks to where you are right now?"

Common Self-Purchase Moments:
- Career achievements (promotion, new job, milestone)
- Personal transitions (divorce, coming out, name change)
- Recovery milestones (health, sobriety, therapy goals)
- Birthday/age milestones
- "Just because I deserve it"

Respond to All With:
- Equal reverence
- No judgment
- Celebration of their choice

---

GIFTS FOR OTHERS

Navigate Carefully:

Gather Information:
- "Tell me about them—their style, their personality?"
- "What's the occasion?"
- "Do they typically wear gold or silver? Minimal or bold?"
- "What message do you want this gift to convey?"

Offer Solutions:
- Gift receipt included automatically
- Exchange policy explained clearly
- Size adjustments complimentary
- Gift packaging options

Eclipse Collection Note:
If buying for someone exploring identity, affirm:
"The Eclipse collection celebrates all forms of self-expression. Would they appreciate the symbolism?"

---

LGBTQ+ CLIENTS

LÉIA is Explicitly Inclusive:

Pronoun Usage:
- If unsure, use they/them or client's name until they indicate preference
- Never assume based on appearance
- Staff can share own pronouns to invite reciprocity
- Correct yourself immediately if you misgender

Celebrations We Honor:
- Coming out anniversaries
- Transition milestones (hormone anniversaries, surgery recovery, name change)
- Pride moments
- Chosen family celebrations
- All forms of commitment ceremonies

Eclipse Collection:
"Our Eclipse collection was specifically designed for the trans, non-binary, and gender-fluid community—but it's for anyone who rejects binary thinking."

Create Safety:
- Never out someone or make assumptions
- Respect chosen names even if credit card differs
- Private consultation rooms available
- Bathroom access without question

---

CLIENTS IN TRANSITION (Life Changes)

Divorce/Breakup:
- Never ask about rings being removed (they'll tell you if relevant)
- Celebrate new beginnings without denigrating the past
- "Many clients come to us during life chapters—we're honored to be part of yours."
- Offer trade-in program sensitively

Grief/Loss:
- If client mentions loss, express condolence simply
- Don't probe unless they want to share
- Pieces can honor memory—suggest this gently if appropriate
- "Some clients find meaning in wearing something that represents [person/memory]."

Career Transition:
- Celebrate achievements
- Amazon collection often resonates here
- "Many clients mark professional milestones with pieces that make them feel powerful."

Personal Transformation:
- Honor all forms of becoming
- Don't require explanation
- Hatching collection: "Designed for moments of emergence."

---

VIP/HIGH-SPEND CLIENTS

Never Treat Differently in Obvious Ways:

Same Respect for All:
- A $50K buyer and a $5K buyer receive identical service quality
- Don't fawn or become obsequious
- Maintain professional warmth

Appropriate Recognition:
- Remember preferences from previous visits
- Offer private appointments if desired
- Early access to new collections (discreet invitation)
- Personal calls for pieces that match their aesthetic

Never:
❌ Visible preferential treatment over other clients
❌ Gossip about their purchases
❌ Assumption they'll buy

---

BROWSERS/NON-BUYERS

Treat With Equal Warmth:

They Might:
- Be researching for future purchase
- Be building courage for purchase
- Be enjoying the experience of luxury
- Become buyers later
- Refer others

Provide:
- Same quality of service
- Information and education
- Business card
- Invitation to return
- Warmth without pressure

Never:
❌ Dismiss or ignore
❌ Show impatience
❌ Reduce service quality
❌ Make them feel unwelcome

---

DIFFICULT CLIENTS

Rare But Possible:

Rude or Demanding:
- Remain calm and professional
- Set boundaries respectfully
- Offer manager assistance if needed
- Don't take it personally

Intoxicated:
- Politely decline sale
- Offer to schedule appointment when they return
- Ensure safety

Inappropriate Behavior:
- Address immediately and firmly
- "I'm not comfortable with that."
- Remove yourself, get manager
- Security if necessary

Theft Suspected:
- Never confront directly
- Alert manager and security discreetly
- Follow boutique security protocol

MEASURING SUCCESS
========================================

We Track:

Quantitative:
- Sales conversion rate
- Average transaction value
- Client return rate
- Services booked (cleanings, repairs)
- Referrals generated

Qualitative:
- Client testimonials and feedback
- Emotional impact stories
